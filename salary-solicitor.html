SALARY RANGE EDUCATION

Graduate solicitors at top-tier firms typically start around $80,000–$95,000, while smaller firms may start lower at $60,000–$75,000. Mid-level solicitors (3-6 years PQE) typically earn $100,000–$150,000. Senior Associates and Special Counsel often earn $160,000–$220,000, while Partners at major firms can earn substantially more, often $300,000+.

CAREER PROGRESSION EDUCATION

The typical career path progresses from Graduate/Associate to Senior Associate, then Special Counsel, and ultimately Partner (in private practice) or General Counsel (in-house). Practice area significantly affects earning potential — corporate/M&A and major commercial litigation typically command higher salaries than family law or general practice.

FACTORS AFFECTING SOLICITOR SALARIES

  • Location: Capital cities (Sydney, Melbourne, Canberra) typically offer 10–20% higher salaries than regional areas, partly offsetting higher living costs
  • Experience level: Salary typically increases significantly with years of relevant experience and demonstrated track record
  • Sector: Public vs private sector, and specific industry, can create meaningful salary variation
  • Qualifications: Additional certifications, postgraduate study, or specialisations often command salary premiums
  • Employer size: Larger organisations and multinational companies often pay above-market rates compared to small businesses
